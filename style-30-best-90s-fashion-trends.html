30 of the Best ’90s Fashion Trends



Nirvana, The Smashing Pumpkins, 2Pac, Beastie Boys, Dr. Dre, Power Rangers, Fresh Prince, Beavis & Butthead... Have nostalgia? Pop culture and music flourished during the 1990s, but fashion wasn't quite as sharp... or was it? Recently, we at CSM have been reflecting on a period that served as our formative years and have found many of the patterns we had forgotten about. Additionally, it seems as though vintage fashions are always making their way back into the contemporary zeitgeist, whether it be through the revival of vintage television series or the continued success of iconic 1990s footwear. Even wearing vintage clothing is becoming a novelty for many.

It is still common to make references to the 1990s while discussing style and street fashion. Major companies like The North Face have brought back coats that were made popular during that beautiful era by numerous East Coast rappers. Due to the continued popularity of '90s fashion, even those outrageous leather jackets by Pelle Pelle and Avirex have recently made a comeback.

We've selected what we think are influential fashion trends to emerge from the 1990s, some of which were fleeting while others are still in use today. Look at our list of the 30 best ’90s fashion styles/trends:



30: Neon Windbreakers

Bright windbreakers resembled magic cloaks. When you put one on, everyone thought you were the coolest man in school. Every type of kid was following this trend, too. The better, the brighter it is.




29: Puffer Jackets

The puffer jacket is still one of the best investments you can make to keep you warm during the coldest winters, and purchasing one is still the perfect way to bring back happy childhood memories and honor your parents' unwavering wisdom.




28: Timberland Boots

Nothing was more pristine than a brand-new pair of 6-inch wheats, and if you polished them with your pencil eraser, you were truly a pro.




27: Mood Rings

So you purchased a mood ring at the mall and occasionally wore it to see if it might truly work. Not at all. And it seemed foolish. But if you were lucky, it led to a few steamy make-out sessions with the hot girl from homeroom and was a wonderful discussion starter.




26: Ripped Jeans

Because if your jeans didn't have at least three holes in them, Nirvana wouldn't sound the same.




25: Gap

The Gap offered the nicest pocket shirts, anoraks, chinos, and everything else you could imagine in every color. The Gap made everyday items hip. You were good to go if you wore only Gap clothing.




24: G-Shock Watches

These days, super-technological timepieces were all the rage, but G-Shocks were little more than a bag of chips. If you had one of these on your wrist as a child, you were unquestionably PHAT.




Wu

Wu Wear, the creation of executive producer Oli "Power" Grant, served only to diversify the bonds of the rapidly expanding group. The variety of leather jackets, cargo shorts, hoodies, shoes, and T-shirts stood out since they all made an effort to include the recognizable "W" emblem.




22: Polo

Ralph never lost popularity, but the Polo Bear and Polo Sport brands enjoyed their heyday in the 1990s. Act as though you are unaware of the blue-bottled cologne. In this era of Polo, the American Flag icon almost equaled the Pony in popularity, and brands like Acapulco Gold were created as a result.




21: Small skateboards

They were created as cruiser boards and work well for commuting. They are lightweight and compact, making them simple to transport in a backpack.




20: Round Sunglasses

These spectacles were bulky and close to the face, yet they were available in a range of contemporary materials, such as tortoiseshell and gold. These glasses were primarily constructed of celluloid but might have wire rims.




19: Air Jordans

In the 1990s, Michael Jordan won SIX titles. By itself, the fact led us to believe that donning Jordans offered us magical abilities. (They still do, too.) During this time, every pair of Js that was released was a hit. Unquestionably the best fashion style of the 1990s.




18: Bandanas

Bandanas were popular among many distinct subcultures throughout the 1990s. They were most notably worn by rappers to signify their gang membership. However, the general public quickly adopted this style and began to wear it as an accessory.




17: Baggy Jeans

In the 1990s, it was generally accepted that the baggier, the better, applied to jeans. The crucial piece for any streetwear ensemble back then were these oversized pants. Additionally, they were frequently worn with a chain that had a Velcro wallet connected at the end and were worn low on the hips.




16: Reebok

We lost it when Reebok appeared on the feet of sportsmen like Michael Chang and Dominique Wilkins. We were completely enthralled by a variety of Reebok styles and still had recent memories of self-lacing shoes. It was amazing to feel like we were watching the footwear of the future take shape.




15: Walkman

The Walkman transformed music's portability years ago. If you wanted to listen to music on the go, this was one of your only options..




14: Skechers

It's incredibly tragic how a brand's reputation can change in a few years from fantastic to not-so-wonderful. Before they started making shoes for women, Skechers was a favorite among men thanks to its assortment of skate shoes, trainers that resembled the Air Max 95, and hybrid shoes that were both dressy and casual.




13: Chain Wallets

Perfect for displaying your badass credentials and for keeping track of where your shit was hidden in the deep pockets of your long-ass jeans.




12: Bleached Hair

The phrase "Suicide Blonde" entered common speech as a result of INXS. And because of Guy Fieri, this craze continues.




11: Skate Tees

Alien Workshop, Hook-Ups, Toy Machine, Thrasher, Spitfire, Girl—the surge in street skating's popularity led to mall rats sporting skate insignia on graphic t-shirts all throughout America. Now that the flannel and chinos uniform has taken hold, you don't see them as frequently.




10: Rollerblades

In-line skating was a close rival to skateboarding before the latter gained popularity among young people. At least half of us recall "getting aggro" in our K2s while hanging out behind the mall since that was how we rocked. And wearing a wristguard truly did make you feel badass.




9: Tommy Hilfiger

Tommy Hilfiger was everything. Guys aspired to be Grand Puba, while girls wanted to look like Aaliyah.




8: Track Jackets

Athletes, grandfathers, B-boys, and British rock stars. All cool. dressed in a track jacket. especially in the three stripes of Adidas.




7: Combat Boots

Before becoming more widely accepted, heavy-duty, lace-up combat boots, like the legendary Dr Martens styles, were preferred by the grunge subculture of the 1990s. For a gritty vintage look, consider wearing them with a pair of ripped jeans and a flannel shirt today.




6: Graphic Tees

Graphic shirts became popular in mainstream fashion thanks to the skate culture of the 1990s. These t-shirts often had a bright placement design on the front and were crew-necked in black or white. Other than the frequently featured skate labels or well-known bands, these prints may be anything.




5: Bucket Hats

Rappers started the bucket hat fad, just like with snapback hats. But, by the end of the decade, every teenager had one in his wardrobe. Similar to bum bags, bucket hats have recently seen a surprising comeback and are once more in style.




4: Turtlenecks

A more preppy alternative to some of the decade's more aggressive fashions were turtleneck sweaters. Boy band members perfected the look, which came in two styles. For sensitive men, there were the thick, warm sweaters, and for the robust, intelligent kinds, there were the dark, fitting, and ribbed garments.




3: Cargo Pants

The 1990s were the decade when cargo pants were in style. These pants were functional and stylish, with so many pockets you could carry your wallet, Gameboy, and even a pager. Although they were originally worn baggy and enormous, narrow or tapered fits tend to look best with today's cargo pants.




2: Snapback Hats

Snapback caps became popular in the 1990s as hip-hop fashion became more widely accepted. Rappers first popularized the style, but soon men all over the world seemed to be sporting it. These flat-brimmed caps, which frequently have logos, can still look chic in modern times. However, keep in mind to wear them forward or backward as opposed to to the side.




1: FILA

The enduring red, blue, and white color scheme of Fila and the embroidered "C" from Champion, both of which have been around for a century, have drawn notice as the demand for timeless, iconic patterns skyrockets.
